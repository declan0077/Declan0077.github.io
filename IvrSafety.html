Overview

As part of my MA in Game Design at Falmouth University, I developed a prototype titled "Enhancing Learning through Gamification: Applying Goal-Setting Theory in IVR" for my final major project. This research-driven initiative explores the intersection of immersive virtual reality (IVR), gamification, and educational theory to revolutionize site safety training. My project addresses a crucial question: "Can integrating Goal-Setting Theory into an immersive virtual reality (IVR) environment enhance learner engagement and effectiveness in site safety training?"

To answer this, I designed and implemented a gamified IVR training module that incorporates clear, attainable goals to intrinsically motivate learners. The prototype simulates various workplace scenarios, allowing users to interact with potential hazards in a safe, controlled environment. By leveraging the immersive nature of VR and the motivational aspects of Goal-Setting Theory, I aimed to create a more engaging and effective learning experience compared to traditional training methods.

A key feature of my approach was the implementation of a progressive Bloom's Taxonomy framework. I designed interactive modules that gradually increase in cognitive complexity, starting from basic recall tasks and advancing to more complex evaluation scenarios. This structured progression aims to deepen learning and enhance knowledge retention across various aspects of site safety. Throughout the development process, I conducted extensive research into learning theories, game design principles, and VR best practices. I then applied these insights to create the interactive modules. The project involved collaboration with industry professionals to ensure the scenarios were realistic and relevant.

This prototype represents a step towards more innovative, engaging, and effective safety training methods. It demonstrates the potential of combining advanced technology with established educational theories to address real-world learning challenges in high-risk industries.
